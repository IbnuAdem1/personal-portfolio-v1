Behind the screen

I’m Ahmed Adem. A while back, I walked away from university to teach myself how to code. It wasn’t about escaping education it was about chasing a quiet obsession and taking complete ownership of how I learn to build things.

I spend my days moving across the stack, but the code is just a tool. For me, development is entirely about the person on the other side of the screen.

"If an experience doesn't satisfy me, I won't hand it over to a user. I don’t ship products just because they are 'done'."

I love the quiet rhythm of problem-solving. I don't just want to build things that work; I want to help make the web a little more open, useful, and intuitive. Hover over the "code" to see the human behind it.
